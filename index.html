Dr. Akinwumi Adesina

The Serving African Son

Here's a time line of Dr. Akinwumi's life:

    • 1960 - Born to a Nigerian farmer in Ibadan, Oyo State Nigeria
    • 1950-1988 - Attended a village school and went on to graduate with a Bachelors degree in Agricultural Economics with First Class Honors from the University of Ife (now Obafemi Awolowo University) in 1981 where he was the first student to be awarded dinstiction by the university. He pursued his studies at Purdue University in Indiana.
    • 1984 - Had to briefly return to Nigera to get married.
    • 1988 - He obtained his PhD (Agricultural Economics) from Purdue where he won the Outstanding PhD Thesis award for his research work.
    • 1990-1995 - He served as a senior economist at West African Rice Development Association(WARDA) in Bouake, Ivory Coast. He worked at the Rockefeller Foundation since winning a fellowship from the Foundation as a senior scientist.
    • 1999-2008 - He served as the representative of the Foundation for the Southern African area and later served as an associate director for food security.
    • 2010-2015 - He was appointed as one of 17 global leaders to spearhead the Millennium Development Goals by the then United Nations Secretary Ban Ki-moon. He served as the Nigerian Minister for Agriculture and was named Forbes Man of the Year for his reform of the Nigerian Agricultural sector. A firm believer in private sector-led growth, he radically changed the perception of agriculture in Nigeria from that of a subsistence to a variable business that sucessfully attracted $5.6 billion in private sector investments. He intoduced more transparency into the fertiliser supply chain.
    • 2015 - He was elected the presumptive President of the African Development Bank and began his tenure of office on the 1 September 2015.
    • 2016 - He was appointed by United Nations Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon to serve as member of the Lead Group of the Scaling Up Nutrition Movement.
    • 2017 - He was awarded 2017 World Food Prize.
    • 2020 - Dr. Adesina was re-elected as President of the African Development Bank for a second term of five years.

    "Dr Adesina has to be commended for his bold leadership on the New Deal on Energy for Africa which would essentially help achieve sustainable energy for all in Africa and also most importantly his global leadership needs to be lauded."
